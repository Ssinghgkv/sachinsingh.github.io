01 — About

Physicist at the quantum frontier

I am a Ph.D. researcher in the Solid State Physics Group at the University of Turin (Italy), where my work centres on understanding how X-ray irradiation modulates the transport properties of MgB₂ superconducting thin films. My thesis explores X-ray-induced effects and the potential of these phenomena for engineering Josephson junctions and radiation-hardened superconducting devices.

My research spans cleanroom microfabrication, photolithography, reactive ion etching (RIE), and cryogenic electrical transport measurements down to 4 K. I have conducted experiments at the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF) in Grenoble, France, using focused nanoscale X-ray beams for site-selective patterning.

Beyond my Ph.D. project, I have gained interdisciplinary experience as a Visiting Researcher at Chipiron (Paris), a start-up developing next-generation ultra-low-field MRI systems using SQUID technology, and at the National Institute of Materials Physics in Bucharest, working on bulk MgB₂ and YBCO thin films.

My background includes M.Sc. research on perovskite solar cell optimization and ZnO thin films, giving me a broad perspective on functional oxide and superconducting material systems.
